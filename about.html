About Me

I'm Elham Hosseinzadeh, an Iranian visual artist and code artist born in 1984. I hold a Master's degree in Painting from Art University of Isfahan, where I graduated top of my class.

My artistic journey bridges traditional visual arts and computational creativity. I create generative art and experimental visuals using Processing, a programming language made for artists and designers. Through code, I explore abstract representations of memory, transformation, and inner emotional landscapes.

My work has been selected by juries in several art competitions and featured in two online exhibitions. I’ve also authored multiple academic articles on visual culture and contemporary art practices.

For me, combining painting and code isn’t just a technical approach — it’s a philosophy. Code allows me to translate the invisible — emotions, shifts in perception, unconscious rhythms — into dynamic, evolving visuals. It’s like painting with logic and light, turning the canvas into a living process.

I believe in poetic technology — the idea that code can be just as expressive, mysterious, and beautiful as a brushstroke. My practice seeks to blur the line between the human and the algorithmic, the intuitive and the calculated.
